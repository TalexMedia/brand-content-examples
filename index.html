01 Start Here

The Six To Look At First

Different categories and budgets. The first two are the closest in shape to the outdoor idea.

Brand + Creator · Garden & Outdoor
From Scratch: The Epic Gardening Way
  • The Home Depot and the gardening creator Kevin Espiritu turn a real property into a working garden and outdoor-living space over the course of the series.
  • Each episode takes on a separate stage of the build, including pathways, fencing, a greenhouse, an outdoor kitchen and the backyard pergola shown here.

The Home Depot x Epic Gardening · Full playlist

Brand + Creator · Workspaces
Make My Space Work
  • The designer Alexandra Gater rebuilds the workspace of a real entrepreneur in each episode, working to a budget and around how that person actually runs their business.
  • Shopify Studios makes the series, and every episode follows the same path from what is not working, through the design plan and the build, to the reveal.

Shopify x Alexandra Gater · Series channel

Brand-Owned Series · Home & Outdoor
The Weekender
  • A host helps one homeowner finish a room or a yard over a single weekend.
  • The series started in 2016 and has run for at least eight seasons across Apple TV, Fire TV, Roku and YouTube. The episode here is a backyard makeover from season eight.

Lowe's · host Monica Mangin · Season 8 playlist

Brand + Creator · Interiors
OMG We're Coming Over
  • A designer and her team redo one creator's real room from top to bottom, filmed from the first walkthrough to the reveal.
  • A different creator features in each episode, and brands including Wayfair and Legrand have supplied the products used in the makeovers.

Mr. Kate · Full playlist · Shorty Awards writeup

Brand-Owned Series · Furniture
IKEA Home Tour
  • Five IKEA employees travel the country and make over rooms in real families' homes, working to whatever problem that particular household has.
  • The first season ran to 28 videos across 13 makeovers. IKEA's own figures put it at 5.6 million views, with store sales up an average of 4.1 percent during the weeks it aired.

IKEA · Full case study and figures

Brand + Creator · DIY · TikTok
To DIY For
  • A DIY competition on TikTok in which creators take on refurbishing challenges using Behr paint, with the weekly winner decided by votes in the comments.
  • It has run for at least three seasons, with a $20,000 prize in season two. By Behr's count the campaign drew 108 million video views and lifted followers by 66 percent.
The series itself, on TikTok

Behr Paint · Case study and results · @behr on TikTok

02 More To Draw From

The Wider Field

Sixteen more, across home, cars, beauty, clothing and software.

Brand + Creator · Automotive
Between 2 Rides
  • Each episode puts two versions of the same car side by side, one left stock and one modified with parts sourced through eBay, then tests them against each other.
  • Season two ran to five episodes with seven builder-creators. eBay counted 22.9 million views, 222,000 new subscribers and more than a million hours watched, and its channel went from sixth to first among auto-parts channels.

eBay Motors, agency The Many · Case study and results

Brand + Creator · DIY
How to Undo
  • The host takes common DIY mistakes and shows how to reverse them, from damaged carpet and badly laid tile to a boring front door.
  • Two seasons and twelve episodes, all shot in eleven business days. Home Depot reported more than a million views, and it took a bronze honor at the Shorty Awards.

The Home Depot x Mike Montgomery, produced by Trade School · Case study

Brand-Owned Series · Interiors
Design of the Times
  • The designer Maurice Harris updates rooms that are stuck in a particular decade while keeping some of the original character in place.
  • There are five episodes, each one built around a recognizable period rather than a general refresh.

Lowe's x Maurice Harris · Full playlist

Brand-Owned Series · Beauty
Make Up Over Makeup
  • Two people with unresolved tension between them do each other's makeup while talking through the relationship.
  • There are two episodes. The first reached 7.6 million views and hit number 16 on YouTube's global trending chart, with no paid media behind it.

e.l.f. Cosmetics · Case study and results

Brand + Creator · Design · TikTok
Redesigning Signs
  • Max Kolomatsky hunts down badly made signs and posters around New York and rebuilds them properly using Adobe tools.
  • Four videos, made with kvell collective. On TikTok they pulled 6.3 million organic views at an 11 percent engagement rate, and the series won Branded Series at the 16th Shorty Awards.

Adobe x Max Kolomatsky · Case study and results

Brand + Creator · Kitchen
IKEA Kitchen Showroom Showdown
  • A competition in which two hosts each design and build their own kitchen under the same set of rules.
  • IKEA Canada made it with the McGillivray Group, running five episodes plus extra social content, and launched it in February 2026.

IKEA Canada, 2026 · IKEA campaign page

Brand-Owned Series · Outdoor
YETI Presents
  • Short documentary films about the kind of people who use YETI gear, including anglers, hunters, cooks and surfers.
  • They are built around personal stories rather than gear, and YETI has released them free on YouTube for close to a decade.

YETI · Film library

Brand + Creator · Travel & Automotive
Wildly Wyoming
  • Three teams of Bronco drivers compete across Wyoming, mixing outdoor challenges with local giveback projects along the route.
  • The shoot covered 28 locations and 1,300 miles across five episodes of 15 to 20 minutes. Outside reported 11.3 million impressions and 8 million video views, and $70,000 went to local organizations.

Ford Bronco Wild Fund, Wyoming Tourism and Outside · Case study and results

Brand-Owned Series · Hospitality
Two Bellmen
  • Scripted action-comedy shorts about two hotel bellmen, set in and around Marriott properties.
  • Three films made by Marriott's own content studio, with the first reportedly passing 5.1 million views.

Marriott · Hollywood Reporter

Brand-Owned Series · Apparel
Living Off the Wall
  • Portraits of skaters, artists, musicians and other people living outside the mainstream.
  • The shoes are barely mentioned, and the subjects wore their own rather than being dressed by the brand.

Vans · Adweek feature

Brand + Creator · Holiday
Merry & Bright
  • Three ten-minute holiday episodes hosted by Jordin Sparks, each one turning a family's home over for Christmas.
  • QR codes on screen let viewers buy the products shown, and it ran on VIZIO's home screen over the 2023 holidays.

The Home Depot with VIZIO, 2023 · Retail Dive

Brand + Creator · DIY · running now
Behr x LaurDIY Dining Room
  • A multi-part sponsored makeover of the creator's own dining room, built around a specific Behr color.
  • Running now on TikTok, posted in parts as the room comes together.

Behr x LaurDIY · Watch on TikTok

Brand + Creator · Furniture
Overhaul
  • A makeover series hosted by Wayfair's creative director, redoing the homes of creators and lifestyle influencers.
  • Viewers could buy the furniture shown through pop-up cards in the video, which made it an early version of shoppable content. It launched in 2015 with the YouTube network Kin Community.

Wayfair, 2015 · Home Accents Today

Brand + Creator · Paint
DIY Influencer Program
  • Eleven designers and DIY creators make over spaces using the brand's paint, built around the annual Color of the Year.
  • A charitable strand runs alongside it, in which the creators repaint shelters and community centers.

Sherwin-Williams · Program page

Brand + Creator · Automotive
Roadkill
  • Two former car-magazine editors buy junk cars, get them running again on camera, and then drive them somewhere far away.
  • The show ran on YouTube from 2012 to 2018, Dodge came on as sponsor in 2015, and a live event called Roadkill Nights grew out of it.

Sponsored by Dodge, 2012 to 2018 · Background

Brand-Owned Series · Small Business
Mailchimp Presents
  • A slate of original films, series and podcasts made for small-business owners, launched with more than 50 titles.
  • Mailchimp produced it with partners including Vice and the Queer Eye production company, and the episodes run five to eight minutes and are free.

Mailchimp, 2019 · Variety

03 Notes On The Numbers

What To Say Carefully

A few figures here are softer than they look.

  • Most of the figures on this page come from the brands' own award entries. They are specific and consistently reported, but they are still self-reported, so they are worth quoting as the brand's stated result rather than as an independent finding.
  • IKEA's 4.1 percent store-sales lift is the number most likely to be questioned, because it claims a sales effect rather than a viewing one.
  • Behr's run length is given as four weeks in one source and six in another, though the view and follower figures match across both.
  • Marriott's view totals and Roadkill's episode numbers come from press write-ups rather than from the brands themselves.
  • On categories, cars are covered here by eBay Motors, Ford and Dodge. Clothing is the thinner one, where brands tend to make their own documentary work rather than pay for a creator's project.
What We Are Proposing
The Same Idea, For The Backyard
What they have in common
In every example here the brand paid for something real to happen, whether that was a garden being built, a car being modified or a film being shot, and the video of it was good enough that people watched it by choice.
The POLYWOOD version
An influencer agency finds a creator who wants to redo their yard, POLYWOOD brings in a designer to plan the space, and we film the whole process from the first walkthrough to the finished reveal.
The format
Each video stands on its own rather than building into a series, so the same setup can run again with a new creator whenever it makes sense. The furniture is simply what the space gets built around.
